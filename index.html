Abstract

This abstract summarizes our entry in an online data science competition on DrivenData.org called “Pump it Up: Data Mining the Water Table”. This project is an initiative in collaboration with Taarifa - aiming at bringing significant health and social impact by ensuring water availability across Tanzania and smoothing maintenance operations. The goal of the competition is to predict the status of water wells in Tanzania in one of the three categories viz. functional, functional needs repair, or not functional. Using the original datasets from the competition, we applied a set of classifiers including k-nearest neighbor, support vector machines, Naive Bayes, Decision trees, and Logistic regression with varying degrees of success. Ultimately, an ensemble classifier produced our best prediction score of 0.8213, placing us 34th out of 1550 teams on the Pump it Up Data science competition leaderboard as of May 25, 2016.

Exploratory Data Analysis

We explore the data with focus on the response variable: 'Operational Status', which is categorical with 3 possible values:
  • functional - the waterpoint is operational and there are no repairs needed
  • functional needs repair - the waterpoint is operational, but needs repairs
  • non functional - the waterpoint is not operational

Waterpoints by Operational Status

The majority of wells, 54.31% are categorized as functional, followed by 38.42% non-functional wells, with a small proportion 7.27% of wells needing repair.

Operational Status by Construction Year

For temporal trends, we examined operational status by construction year. Newer wells tend to show a higher proportion of functional status and older wells tend to have higher proportions of non-functional status. What is somewhat surprising is that wells in need of repair do not show a clear pattern with respect to year built. We also note that the time in which the proportion of functional to non-functional wells intersect is between 1985 and 1990, possibly indicating that an average well life may be around 25 years

Results as seen through ROC curves

Above are the Receiver Operating Characteristic (ROC) curves for each of the component models we used in our ensemble. They show that the models had the most difficulty with accurately predicting the third status: ‘functional needs repair’. By combining these into an ensemble we were able to compensate for their individual weaknesses.
